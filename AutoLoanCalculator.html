Professional Auto Loan Formulas (Complete Reference)

1. Net Loan Amount Calculation

Net Loan = (Vehicle Price - Down Payment - Trade-in Value) × (1 + Sales Tax Rate/100)

Accounts for sales tax applied to the net purchase price (standard industry practice).

2. Monthly Payment Formula (Amortizing Loan)

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1]
Where:
M = Monthly Payment
P = Principal Loan Amount
r = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate/12/100)
n = Total Number of Payments (Years × 12)

Standard amortization formula used by financial institutions for auto loans.

3. Total Interest Paid Formula

Total Interest = (M × n) - P
Effective Interest Rate = (Total Interest / P) / Loan Term (years) × 100

Calculates total interest over the life of the loan and effective annual rate.

4. Early Payoff Formula

New Monthly Payment = Original M + Extra Payment
New Number of Payments = log(M/(M - P×r)) / log(1+r)
Interest Savings = Original Total Interest - New Total Interest

Logarithmic formula to calculate reduced loan term with extra payments.

5. Monthly Amortization Formula

Monthly Interest = Remaining Balance × Monthly Rate
Monthly Principal = Monthly Payment - Monthly Interest
New Balance = Previous Balance - Monthly Principal

Month-by-month breakdown used in official amortization schedules.
