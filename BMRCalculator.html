Scientific BMR Formulas (Complete Reference)

1. Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (Most Accurate - 2005)

Men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) + 5 Women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) - 161 Other/Non-Binary: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) - 78

Considered the gold standard for BMR calculation with ±10% accuracy for most populations.

2. Revised Harris-Benedict Equation (1990)

Men: BMR = (13.397 × weight in kg) + (4.799 × height in cm) - (5.677 × age) + 88.362 Women: BMR = (9.247 × weight in kg) + (3.098 × height in cm) - (4.330 × age) + 447.593

Updated version of the classic 1919 equation, better suited for modern populations.

3. Schofield Equation (WHO Standard - 1985)

Men (18-30): BMR = (15.057 × weight in kg) + (692.2) Men (30-60): BMR = (11.472 × weight in kg) + (873.1) Women (18-30): BMR = (14.818 × weight in kg) + (486.6) Women (30-60): BMR = (8.126 × weight in kg) + (845.6)

World Health Organization standard, age-stratified for better accuracy.

4. Owen Equation (Lean Individuals - 1986)

Men: BMR = (8.783 × weight in kg) + 829.1 Women: BMR = (7.26 × weight in kg) + 795.0

Optimized for lean individuals with low body fat percentage.

5. TDEE Calculation Formula (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier Activity Multipliers: - Sedentary (little exercise): 1.2 - Light activity (light exercise/sports 1-3 days/week): 1.375 - Moderate activity (moderate exercise/sports 3-5 days/week): 1.55 - Very active (hard exercise/sports 6-7 days a week): 1.725 - Extra active (very hard exercise/sports & physical job): 2.4

Calculates total daily calorie needs including physical activity.

6. Weight Management Calorie Target Formulas

Weight Maintenance: Calorie Target = TDEE Weight Loss (0.5kg/week): Calorie Target = TDEE - 500 Weight Loss (1kg/week): Calorie Target = TDEE - 1000 Weight Gain (0.5kg/week): Calorie Target = TDEE + 500 Weight Gain (1kg/week): Calorie Target = TDEE + 1000 Note: 1 kg body weight ≈ 7700 calories (3500 calories per lb)

Evidence-based calorie adjustments for safe weight management.

7. Macronutrient Distribution Formulas

Protein: (Body Weight in kg × Protein Factor) g/day (4 calories per gram) Fat: 25-30% of total calories (9 calories per gram) Carbs: Remaining calories (4 calories per gram) Example: 2000 calorie diet, 70kg person, 1.8g/kg protein - Protein: 70 × 1.8 = 126g = 504 calories (25.2%) - Fat: 2000 × 0.25 = 500 calories = 55.6g (25%) - Carbs: 2000 - 504 - 500 = 996 calories = 249g (49.8%)

Optimal macronutrient split for balanced nutrition.

Accuracy Notes & Limitations

  • BMR calculations are estimates (actual metabolic rate varies by 10-15% between individuals)
  • Equations don't account for body composition (muscle mass increases BMR significantly)
  • Hormonal factors (thyroid function), medications, and medical conditions affect true BMR
  • Mifflin-St Jeor equation is recommended for general use (most validated in modern populations)
  • For clinical accuracy, indirect calorimetry (metabolic cart) is required (gold standard)

Conversion Factors (Metric ↔ Imperial)

  • 1 kilogram (kg) = 2.20462 pounds (lb)
  • 1 pound (lb) = 0.453592 kilograms (kg)
  • 1 centimeter (cm) = 0.393701 inches (in)
  • 1 inch (in) = 2.54 centimeters (cm)
  • 1 pound of body fat = 3500 calories
  • 1 kilogram of body fat = 7700 calories
