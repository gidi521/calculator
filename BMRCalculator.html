Understanding Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): Features, Usage, and More


Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulation, and cell production. Knowing your BMR can help you manage your diet and fitness plan more effectively.


Introduction to BMR:


BMR is a measure of the energy expended by the body at rest to maintain vital bodily functions. It varies based on age, gender, weight, and height. Understanding your BMR helps you estimate the minimum calorie intake required to sustain your body's basic physiological needs.


How BMR is Calculated:


The Harris-Benedict equation is commonly used to estimate BMR:

For men: BMR = 10 * weight (kg) + 6.25 * height (cm) - 5 * age (years) + 5

For women: BMR = 10 * weight (kg) + 6.25 * height (cm) - 5 * age (years) - 161
