What Is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body burns at rest to keep vital functions working, including breathing, cell production, nutrient processing, and organ function. BMR represents about 60–75% of your total daily calories burned.

What Is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is your BMR multiplied by your activity level. It represents the total calories you burn in an average day. TDEE is the most important number for weight management.

How It Works

  • Maintenance: Eat = TDEE → weight stays the same
  • Fat Loss: Eat < TDEE → body uses stored fat
  • Muscle Gain: Eat > TDEE → body builds muscle

Activity Levels Explained

  • Sedentary: Little to no exercise, desk job
  • Light Activity: 1–3 days/week light exercise
  • Moderate Activity: 3–5 days/week moderate exercise
  • Active: 6–7 days/week hard exercise
  • Very Active: Physical labor + daily training

Calorie Goals Explained

  • Maintain: TDEE → no gain, no loss
  • Mild Weight Loss: TDEE − 300 → safe, sustainable
  • Fast Weight Loss: TDEE − 500 → 0.5kg per week
  • Muscle Gain: TDEE + 200–300 → clean bulking

Why 3500 Calories = 0.5kg Fat

One kilogram of body fat equals approximately 7700 calories. This means a daily deficit of 500 calories leads to about 0.5kg of fat loss per week — the gold standard for healthy, sustainable weight loss.

Scientific Formula Used

Male BMR: 88.362 + (13.397 × weight) + (4.799 × height) − (5.677 × age)

Female BMR: 447.593 + (9.247 × weight) + (3.098 × height) − (4.330 × age)
